About me:

So why Management of Technology?

After I discovered my interest in the STEM field at 5th grade I naturally found myself graduating as a Mechatronics engineer from BME. However, during my bachelor years I realized that putting together something truly remarkable requires more than one person, so I particpated in a lot of team projects.

During these projects I found out that while engineering is hard on it's own, managing high-performing engineers is even harder! Attracted by the challange, I followed my newfound interest in leadership and project management, and submitted my application to the the TU Delft Management of Technology programme.

In the past couple years I learnt quite a lot about innovation management, but I realized that many decisions aren't based on accurate data. Therefore I plan to finish my studies by researching what inhibits the full adoption of data science into strategy the creation process, and what can we do about it.
